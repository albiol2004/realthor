1. CRITICAL FOR A DEAL (Críticos)

Cannot sign at Notary, cannot legally advertise, or puts Agent at high legal risk.

    IDENTIFICATION: DNI/NIE/Passport, Power of Attorney (if applicable).[15][16]

    AML (BLANQUEO): KYC Form, Proof of Funds (Vital for the agent's compliance).

    DEEDS: Property Title Deed, Nota Simple (Must be recent).

    CERTIFICATES:

        Energy Certificate (CEE) (Required by law to simply list the property).

        Community Debt Certificate (Notary will demand this to sign).[21]

        Habitability Certificate (Cédula) (Critical in regions like Catalonia/Navarre/Asturias to sign).

    TAX: IBI Receipt (Proof of payment).[8][12][15]

    CONTRACTS: Sales Listing Agreement (Nota de Encargo) (Without this, you have no right to sell or claim commission).

    NEW BUILD: Seguro Decenal (If house is <10 years), Libro del Edificio.

2. LEGALLY RECOMMENDED (Legalmente Recomendados)

Required to avoid future fines, tax blocks, or "surprises" that kill the deal last minute.

    LEGAL/URBAN: Certificate of No Urban Infraction (Highly recommended to ensure no illegal extensions exist that the City Hall could fine/demolish).[9]

    CONTRACTS: Earnest Money Contract (Arras) (Protects the deal before Notary).[15]

    TECHNICAL: Technical Building Inspection (ITE) (Mandatory for older buildings; banks may deny mortgage without it).

    UTILITIES: Electrical Bulletin (CIE) (Often needed to change electricity name if the old one expired).

    TAX: Plusvalía Municipal (Seller must prove payment eventually).[8][15]

    RENTALS: Solvency Documents (Payslips/Tax returns) & Rent Default Insurance.

3. ADVISED (Aconsejados)

Good practice, due diligence, and marketing.

    COMMUNITY: Meeting Minutes (Actas) (To check for hidden costs/bad neighbors), Statutes.

    PLANS: Floor Plans, Cadastral Plans.

    UTILITIES: Past Utility Bills (To analyze consumption costs).

    INSURANCE: Home Insurance (Unless mortgage requires it).

    PHOTOS: Defect photos.
